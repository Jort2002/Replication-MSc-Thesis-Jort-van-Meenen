# Informed Consent Form

**Research Study: Early Value in AI Automation Builders for Non-Technical Users**
Utrecht University — Department of Information and Computing Science | Researcher: Jort van Meenen
*Design Cycle 2 — High-Fidelity Prototype User Study*

---

## Purpose of the Study

You are invited to participate in a research study conducted as part of a Master's thesis in Business Informatics at Utrecht University. The purpose of this study is to understand the challenges that non-technical professionals encounter when first using AI automation builders (such as Zapier or Make), and to develop and evaluate user experience design principles that support early value realization in such systems. In this session, you will use a functional web-based prototype of such a system and complete two realistic tasks while thinking aloud.

## What Participation Involves

Your participation involves a single session lasting approximately 45 to 60 minutes, conducted remotely via video conferencing. The session consists of two tasks:

1. **Task 1 — Create an automation:** Using the prototype, you will set up an automated workflow based on a scenario provided by the researcher. You are encouraged to think aloud throughout.
2. **Task 2 — Manage an automation:** You will pause an existing automation, make an adjustment, and re-activate it.

After completing the tasks, you will fill in a short questionnaire covering usability (System Usability Scale), perceived usefulness and ease of use (Technology Acceptance Model), and your sense of control over the system. The session will be screen- and audio-recorded. The researcher will observe and take structured notes.

## Voluntary Participation

Your participation is entirely voluntary. You may decline to answer any question, skip any task, and you may withdraw at any time without providing a reason and without any negative consequences. If you choose to withdraw, any data collected from your participation will be deleted.

## Confidentiality and Data Handling

All data collected during this study will be treated confidentially and in accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). The following measures will be taken to protect your privacy:

- Your name and any identifying information will be anonymized. You will be referred to by a two-letter pseudonym (e.g., P1, P2) in all research materials.
- Screen and audio recordings will be stored securely and deleted after analysis is completed.
- Questionnaire responses will be stored anonymously and cannot be traced back to you individually.
- Transcripts and analysis files will be stored on a password-protected device accessible only to the researcher and supervisors.
- Anonymized excerpts may be included in the thesis and in any resulting academic publications.
- Anonymized transcripts and session materials may be made available in a publicly accessible open-access replication package associated with this thesis. Before any materials are shared publicly, identifying details (such as employer, organisation, and other re-identifying context) are removed or generalized.
- Data will be retained for a maximum of one year after completion of the thesis, after which it will be permanently deleted.

## Risks and Benefits

There are no anticipated risks associated with participation beyond those encountered in everyday professional use of software tools. While there are no direct personal benefits, your participation contributes to academic research aimed at improving the usability and accessibility of AI automation tools for non-technical users.
