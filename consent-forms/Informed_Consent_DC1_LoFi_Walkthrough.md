# Informed Consent Form

**Research Study: Early Value in AI Automation Builders for Non-Technical Users**
Utrecht University — Department of Information and Computing Science | Researcher: Jort van Meenen
*Design Cycle 1 — Low-Fidelity Prototype Walkthrough*

---

## Purpose of the Study

You are invited to participate in a research study conducted as part of a Master's thesis in Business Informatics at Utrecht University. The purpose of this study is to understand the challenges that non-technical professionals encounter when first using AI automation builders (such as Zapier or Make), and to develop and evaluate user experience design principles that support early value realization in such systems. In this session, you will be shown an early, low-fidelity prototype of such a system and asked for your impressions.

## What Participation Involves

Your participation involves a single session lasting approximately 40 to 50 minutes, conducted remotely via video conferencing. During the session, the researcher will share their screen and present the prototype one screen at a time. You will not operate the prototype yourself; instead, you will be asked to think aloud and share your first impressions of each screen: what is clear, what is confusing, and what you would expect to happen next. The session concludes with a few short reflection questions. The session will be audio-recorded and transcribed for analysis.

## Voluntary Participation

Your participation is entirely voluntary. You may decline to answer any question, and you may withdraw at any time without providing a reason and without any negative consequences. If you choose to withdraw, any data collected from your participation will be deleted.

## Confidentiality and Data Handling

All data collected during this study will be treated confidentially and in accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). The following measures will be taken to protect your privacy:

- Your name and any identifying information will be anonymized. You will be referred to by a single-letter pseudonym (e.g., B, C) in all research materials.
- Audio recordings will be stored securely and deleted after transcription and analysis are completed.
- Transcripts and analysis files will be stored on a password-protected device accessible only to the researcher and supervisors.
- Anonymized excerpts may be included in the thesis and in any resulting academic publications.
- Anonymized transcripts may be made available in a publicly accessible open-access replication package associated with this thesis. Before any transcript is shared publicly, identifying details (such as employer, organisation, and other re-identifying context) are removed or generalized.
- Data will be retained for a maximum of one year after completion of the thesis, after which it will be permanently deleted.

## Risks and Benefits

There are no anticipated risks associated with participation beyond those encountered in everyday professional discussion. While there are no direct personal benefits, your participation contributes to academic research aimed at improving the usability and accessibility of AI automation tools for non-technical users.
